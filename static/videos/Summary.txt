This video presents ISR (Information-Standardized Trajectory
Resampling), an offline preprocessing method that resamples robotic
teleoperation demonstrations to achieve uniform kinematic-dynamic
information density. The first part of the video introduces the ISR
method architecture and experimental results, covering a baseline
comparison, an ablation study on the acceleration weight, and a
cross-operator robustness analysis across three real-world
manipulation tasks. The second part shows real-world inference
rollouts of the VLA policy pi_0.5 trained on
ISR-resampled datasets, demonstrating task execution on Place&Cover,
Place&Stack, and Push-T using an AgileX Piper robot arm.
